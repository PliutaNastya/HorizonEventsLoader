The first written reference occurs in the eighth century, in one of the earliest Japanese texts.

In the myth, a god named Izanagi travels to the netherworld to find his wife, Izanami. This was considered a taboo journey at the time.

As Izanagi returned from his dangerous voyage, he took time to stop and bath, thereby purifying and transforming himself.

As the centuries passed, these acts of purification and transformation became more adventurous, and thus, the practice of Misogi was born.

Ascetic practitioners took an annual pilgrimage around the mountains of medieval Japan, challenging themselves in unique and uncommon ways.

Athletes and Entrepreneurs have adapted this “concept further and revered an Annual Misogi’s impact on their life…”
